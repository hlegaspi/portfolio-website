Playback: 80's Music App

My role in this project was the UI/UX designer and the duration of the project was 1 month. The purpose of the project was to design a music mobile app that is familiar to users and has a cohesive UI throughout the app. The objective was establish key functionalities and design the UI for an 80’s music player mobile app.

My Process

Research

An analysis of two well-known music apps, Spotify and Soundcloud, was conducted to better understand common usability heuristics, design principles, and UI patterns. This information was then applied to the app’s design to create familiarity and ease of navigation for the user. Examples of elements to be incorporated in the design include an onboarding process (login /sign up), navigation bar, search, playlist, library, profile and social patterns.

Userflow

I created a userflow that would show the different ways users can reach each screen throughout the mobile app.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

After establishing the key functionalities (i.e. Create an Account, Search for New Songs, Make a Playlist, Follow other Profiles, and Like Songs), I drew every screen that would show how a user would achieve each task.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

I then moved on to AdobeXD to digitalize and refine the low fidelity wireframes. I established a hierarchy (without adding any typography or color palettes) to create an organized layout for the app.

Moodboard

The app’s visual design was inspired by the 80’s, but specifically polaroid photos and the Polaroid brand. This led to the idea of incorporating Swiss Design into the UI. The overall aesthetic was to be nostalgic, modern, and minimal.

Style Guide

The color palette is minimal utilizing black and white. This decision was made so that the colors did not interfere with the images associated with songs, playlists, or profiles. Also, it helps emphasize the nostalgic feel the app was going for, while still being modern. The font used is Neue Haas Grotesk Display Pro due to the Swiss Design influence and also to keep the app modern and minimal. Black and white photographs are cropped in various squares throughout the app’s UI to emulate polaroid photos and emphasize the app’s overall theme.

Final Screens

The final product shows each main screen that the user can visit. The onboarding screen allows the user to either login or signup. The home screen allows the users to easily visit popular, recently played, most played, and/or recommended music. Then, there’s a search screen that allows users to find songs they’d like to add, like, or listen to. Users can also click on the add screen to create a playlist. They can also visit their music library to find their songs and playlists. Lastly, users can view the profile screen to view their followers and those they follow.

Conclusion

I’ve learned that designs evolve over time and that iterations are part of the design process. Also, it’s important to stay organized and establish main components ahead of time. Lastly, as designers it’s important to continue to look for inspiration and that inspiration can be found in the everyday things.
